# Privacy Settings Overview

Tavrn gives you real control over your privacy. These aren't checkbox-theater settings that don't do anything, they actually determine who sees what.

Find them all at **Settings → Privacy & Safety**.

---

## The Full List of Privacy Controls

### 👤 Profile Visibility

Controls who can view your profile details (bio, location, etc.).

| Option | Who can see your profile |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | Everyone on Tavrn |
| **Friends Only** | Only people on your friends list |
| **Only Me** | Nobody else (just your name and avatar are visible) |

<Tip>
  Even on "Private," your display name and avatar are visible in rooms you're in, people can still identify you in shared spaces. What's hidden is the details: bio, social links, pronouns, etc.
</Tip>

---

### 👥 Friend List Visibility

Controls whether other people can see your friends list.

| Option | Who can see who you're friends with |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | Everyone |
| **Friends Only** | Only your friends |
| **Only Me** | Nobody |

---

### 🟢 Presence / Online Status Visibility

Controls who can see your online status dot (online, away, busy, offline).

| Option | Who sees your status |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | All Tavrn users |
| **Friends** | Only your friends |
| **Nobody** | You always appear offline to everyone |

<Note>
  "Nobody" makes you appear fully offline to everyone, including close friends. If you want to be invisible to strangers but visible to friends, use "Friends."
</Note>

---

### 💬 Direct Messages

Controls who can send you DMs.

| Option | Who can message you |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | Any Tavrn user |
| **Friends** | Only people on your friends list |
| **Nobody** | No one can DM you |

---

### 🤝 Friend Requests

Controls who can send you friend requests.

| Option | Who can add you |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | Any Tavrn user |
| **Friends of Friends** | Only people who share a mutual friend with you |
| **Nobody** | No new friend requests at all |

---

### 🔍 Search Visibility

Controls whether you show up in Tavrn's user search.

| Option | Who can find you by searching |
|---|---|
| **Everyone** | Any Tavrn user searching by username |
| **Friends** | Only existing friends |
| **Nobody** | You don't appear in search results at all |

<Warning>
  Setting search visibility to "Nobody" means people who don't already know you can't find you. This is a strong privacy measure, but it also means people legitimately trying to reconnect with you won't be able to find you through search.
</Warning>

## Notification Settings

The **Notifications** tab in Settings (separate from Privacy & Safety) has toggles for:
- **Push Notifications**, Receive browser/device notifications when you're not actively in the app
- **Email Summaries**, A digest of missed activity sent to your email (daily or weekly)

## Recommended Configurations

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Open & Social">
    Good for creators, community managers, or anyone who wants to be discoverable:
    - Profile Visibility: **Everyone**
    - Friend List Visibility: **Friends Only**
    - Presence: **Everyone**
    - DMs: **Everyone**
    - Friend Requests: **Everyone**
    - Search: **Everyone**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Friends-First">
    Balanced privacy, visible to friends, guarded against strangers:
    - Profile Visibility: **Friends Only**
    - Friend List Visibility: **Friends Only**
    - Presence: **Friends Only**
    - DMs: **Friends Only**
    - Friend Requests: **Everyone**
    - Search: **Everyone**
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Maximum Privacy">
    For those who prefer near-total privacy:
    - Profile Visibility: **Only Me**
    - Friend List Visibility: **Only Me**
    - Presence: **Nobody**
    - DMs: **Friends Only**
    - Friend Requests: **Nobody**
    - Search: **Nobody**
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## These Settings Don't Affect Room Participation

Your privacy settings control your *personal* visibility, profile, status, DMs, friend requests. They don't affect your presence in rooms you've joined. People in a shared room will still see your messages and that you're a member, regardless of privacy settings.

If you want to be invisible in a room, you'd need to leave it.
