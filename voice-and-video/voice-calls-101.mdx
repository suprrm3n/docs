# Voice Calls 101

One of the best things about Tavrn is how easy it is to hop on a call. Here's everything you need to know about voice calls.

---

## Two Types of Voice on Tavrn

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="📞 DM Calls" icon="phone">
    1-on-1 voice or video calls with a specific friend. You initiate it from a DM conversation.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🔊 Room Voice Channels" icon="mic">
    Group hangout spaces inside a room. Anyone in the room can drop in and out freely.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Both work on the same underlying technology, Cloudflare's real-time infrastructure, and both support voice, video, and screen sharing.

---

## Starting a DM Call

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open a DM conversation">
    Head to your DMs and click on the friend you want to call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the call button">
    Look for the 📞 phone icon at the top-right of the conversation. Click it to start a voice call.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for them to answer">
    Your friend will receive an incoming call notification with an audio cue. They can accept or decline.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You're connected!">
    Once they accept, the call connects and your voices come through in real time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Joining a Room Voice Channel

Voice channels in rooms work differently from calls, they're always open, and you just walk in.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a voice channel in the channel list">
    Voice channels have a 🔊 icon. You can see who's already in a voice channel before joining.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the channel name to join">
    You'll be instantly connected. No ringing, no waiting, just join.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Talk!">
    Your microphone is active by default. Mute yourself with the mute button in the call bar if you need to.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Receiving a Call

When someone calls you in a DM:

- You'll hear an **audio notification** (an incoming call sound)
- A **pop-up toast notification** appears with the caller's name and avatar
- You can **Accept** or **Decline** from the notification

If you're currently in another call, you'll still see the incoming call notification, you can choose to switch or decline.

---

## The Active Call Bar

Once you're in a call, a persistent **Active Call Bar** appears at the bottom of the screen. This stays visible as you navigate around Tavrn, you'll always be able to see your call status and control it.

From the call bar, you can:
- **Mute/unmute** your microphone
- **Enable/disable** your camera
- **Share your screen**
- **Hang up** the call
- **Open the full call view** to see video, participants, etc.

---

## Leaving a Call

**To end a DM call:** Click the red hang-up button in the call bar or the call overlay.

**To leave a room voice channel:** Click the disconnect button (usually a ✕ or a leave icon) in the call bar. Other people in the channel will remain, you're just stepping out.

---

## Call Duration

For DM calls, Tavrn tracks the duration so you can see how long you talked. This shows up in the DM conversation.

---

## Call Quality

Tavrn's voice technology uses Cloudflare's edge infrastructure, meaning your audio and video are routed through rooms geographically close to you. This keeps latency low and quality high.

If you're experiencing call quality issues, see [Troubleshooting Calls](/voice-and-video/troubleshooting-calls).

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I call someone who isn't my friend?">
    DM calls are only available between people who have an open DM conversation. You generally need to be friends (or have at least started messaging) to call someone.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I call someone who's offline?">
    No. Voice calls require both people to be connected to Tavrn in real time. If someone is offline, the call won't go through.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a time limit on calls?">
    No. There's no time limit. Late-night sessions with friends, long-distance calls, and all-day study sessions are all fine.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use headphones instead of speakers?">
    Yes, and we recommend it! Headphones reduce echo and feedback, especially in group calls. Tavrn uses whatever audio device your system defaults to. You can change that in **Settings > Voice & Video**.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I join a voice call on mobile?">
    Yes. Voice calls work on mobile browsers. For the best experience, use a headset or earbuds to avoid echo.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
