# Managing Your Friends List

You've got the basics down. This article goes deeper into the friends system, managing requests, reading mutual connections, and keeping things organized.

---

## Your Friends List at a Glance

Open **Friends** in the sidebar to see everyone you're connected with. The list is sorted by status:

1. 🟢 **Online** friends at the top
2. 🟡 **Away/Idle** friends next
3. 🔴 **Busy** friends after that
4. ⚫ **Offline** friends at the bottom

Each entry shows their avatar, display name, and current status (including custom status message if they've set one).

---

## Pending Requests Tab

The **Pending** tab in the Friends section shows two things:

**Incoming requests**, People who have sent *you* a friend request. From here you can Accept or Decline.

**Outgoing requests**, Requests *you* have sent that haven't been responded to yet. You'll see them here until the other person accepts or declines.

<Note>
  If you decline someone's request, they won't receive a notification. From their perspective, the request is just still pending. They can't tell whether you declined or just haven't seen it yet.
</Note>

---

## Mutual Friends

When you view someone's profile, Tavrn shows you the **friends you have in common**. This is useful for:

- Understanding how someone found you
- Getting a quick read on who they are from who you both know
- Finding new people to add through shared connections

Mutual friends are visible to both parties, it's not hidden information.

---

## Mutual Rooms

Similarly, viewing a profile shows which **rooms you're both in**. This is handy context before sending a DM to someone, you can see right away where you've overlapped.

---

## Removing a Friend

Friendships sometimes run their course. To remove someone:

1. Open the **Friends** section
2. Find their name (or open their profile)
3. Click **⋯ more options** → **Remove Friend**

No notification is sent to them. Your DM conversation history stays intact, you just won't be friends anymore.

---

## The Difference Between Remove, Block, and Ignore

| Action | They can still DM you | They can see your status | They can send friend requests | You see their messages in rooms |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| **Remove friend** | Maybe (depends on DM settings) | Depends on privacy settings | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| **Block** | ❌ No | ❌ No | ❌ No | Hidden |

<Info>
  Learn more about blocking in [Blocking Someone](/profile-and-social/blocking-someone).
</Info>

## Who Can Send You Friend Requests?

This is controlled in **Settings → Privacy & Safety & Safety → Friend Requests**:

- **Everyone**, Anyone on Tavrn can send you a request
- **Friends**, Only friends of your existing friends (people you have in common)
- **Nobody**, No one can send you new requests

Locking it to "nobody" is useful if you're a creator or public figure who'd rather have people DM you through a room or other channel.

---

## Can Friends See My Activity?

Friends can see your **online status** and **custom status message**, unless you've restricted those in your privacy settings. They can also see your full profile (unless it's locked to "private").

They cannot see:
- Who else you're friends with (unless your friend list is set to public)
- What DMs you're in
- What you're typing

---

## Friend Count Visibility

Your friend list visibility is a separate privacy setting:

- **Public**, Anyone can see your friends list
- **Friends**, Only your friends can see who else you're friends with
- **Private**, Your friends list is hidden from everyone

Set this in **Settings → Privacy & Safety & Safety → Friend List Visibility**.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How do I know if someone removed me as a friend?">
    You won't receive a notification. You might notice they're no longer in your friends list, or that you can't see their status anymore. Tavrn doesn't make a big deal out of friend removals.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can someone send me multiple friend requests?">
    If you decline a request, the person can theoretically send another one. If someone is repeatedly sending you unwanted requests, blocking them is the right move.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a limit to how many friends I can have?">
    No hard cap. Add as many friends as you'd like.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="If I remove someone and they remove me, is there a cooldown on re-adding?">
    No cooldown. Either party can send a new friend request at any time after a removal (unless one person has blocked the other).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
