# Roles & Permissions

Roles are how room owners manage who can do what inside their community. Understanding the role system helps you both as a member and as a room owner.

---

## The Default Role Hierarchy

Tavrn rooms have five built-in roles, from most powerful to least:

<CardGroup cols={1}>
  <Card title="👑 Owner" icon="crown">
    **The room creator.** Has complete control over every aspect of the room, settings, channels, members, moderation, and more. Can delete the room. There is only ever one owner per room.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🛡️ Admin" icon="shield">
    **Trusted leaders.** Admins can manage most things, channels, categories, invite links, room settings, roles (below admin), and member management (kick, ban). Can't delete the room or modify other admins.
  </Card>
  <Card title="⚔️ Moderator" icon="gavel">
    **Community keepers.** Moderators can kick members and delete messages. Can't change room settings or manage channels.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🤖 TavBot" icon="cpu">
    **The automated assistant.** TavBot is Tavrn's built-in moderation bot, it has its own role in the member list. Room admins control what TavBot does via the Moderation tab in room settings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🧑 Member" icon="user">
    **The default role.** Can read and send messages in accessible channels, join voice channels, react to messages, and participate normally. Can't change anything about the room itself.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Permission Breakdown

Here's a detailed look at what each role can do:

| Action | Member | Moderator | Admin | Owner |
|---|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Read messages | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Send messages | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Join voice channels | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| React to messages | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Delete own messages | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Delete others' messages | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Kick members | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Ban members | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Manage channels | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Manage room settings | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Create invite links | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Assign roles (up to own level) | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |
| Transfer ownership | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |
| Delete room | ❌ | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ |

## Assigning Roles

Only **Admins** and the **Owner** can assign roles to other members.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Room Settings → Members">
    Navigate to your room settings and find the Members list.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click on a member">
    Find the member you want to update and click their name.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Change their role">
    Select the new role from the dropdown.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Changes take effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can only assign roles at or below your own level. An Admin can make someone a Moderator, but not another Admin. Only the Owner can promote someone to Admin.
</Note>

## Kicking vs. Banning

**Kick**, Removes the member from the room. They can rejoin if they have a valid invite link. Use this for lighter situations or first offenses.

**Ban**, Permanently removes the member and prevents them from rejoining via invite. Use this for serious or repeated violations.

Both actions are logged and visible to admins.

## Tips for Room Owners

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="How many admins should I have?">
    Keep the admin team small and trusted. More admins doesn't mean better moderation, it often means more potential for accidental misuse. Start with 1-2 trusted admins and expand if the room grows to need it.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Should I have mods separate from admins?">
    Yes, for larger rooms. Mods handle the day-to-day (deleting messages, cooling off heated conversations, welcoming members). Admins handle the structure (channels, settings, escalated bans). This division keeps things running smoothly.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if a moderator is abusing their power?">
    As owner or admin, you can revoke moderator status at any time. Remove the role in Room Settings → Members.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I create custom roles beyond the four defaults?">
    Currently, Tavrn uses the four standard roles. Custom role creation is a commonly requested feature, stay tuned.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## As a Regular Member: Understanding Your Permissions

If you're a new member of a room and something isn't available to you (you can't see a channel, can't send messages in a specific place, etc.), it's usually because:

1. **You don't have the right role yet**, Ask an admin if this is intentional and if there's a way to get elevated access.
2. **The channel is restricted**, Some channels are only for certain roles (mods-only, announcements, etc.).
3. **You haven't been verified**, Some rooms require new members to agree to rules before getting full access.

<Tip>
  If you join a room and can barely see any channels, look for a **#verify** or **#welcome** channel. Many rooms require new members to agree to rules before unlocking the full community.
</Tip>
