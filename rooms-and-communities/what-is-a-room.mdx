# What is a Room?

If you're new to platforms like Tavrn (or Discord, for that matter), the term "room" might seem weird. Nothing to worry about, it's simpler than it sounds.

---

## The Short Version

A **room** is a dedicated community space. It has a name, an icon, its own channels, and its own members. Think of it like a clubhouse, each room is its own little world that exists within Tavrn.

You can be a member of many rooms simultaneously. Switch between them by clicking their icons in your left sidebar.

---

## What's Inside a Room

Every room contains:

  <Card title="💬 Text Channels" icon="hash">
    For written conversations. Each channel typically has a specific topic, like `#general`, `#gaming`, or `#memes`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="🔊 Voice Channels" icon="mic">
    Hang out and talk in real-time. You can see who's currently in a voice channel before you join.
  </Card>
  <Card title="👥 Members" icon="users">

  </Card>

## Channels, Categories, and Organization

Channels are organized into **categories**, collapsible groups that cluster related channels together.

For example, a gaming room might look like this:

```
📁 WELCOME & INFO
  📌 #rules
  📢 #announcements
  
📁 GENERAL
  # general-chat
  # off-topic
  # memes
  
📁 GAMING
  # fortnite
  # minecraft
  # looking-for-group
  🔊 Gaming Voice 1
  🔊 Gaming Voice 2
  
📁 CHILL ZONE
  # music-chat
  🔊 Chill Lounge
```

Channels with a `#` are text channels. Channels with a 🔊 are voice channels.

<Tip>
  On Tavrn, channel categories are collapsible, you can hide categories you don't use much to keep the sidebar clean.
</Tip>

## Roles & Hierarchy

Everyone in a room has a **role** that defines their permissions. The default roles from most to least powerful are:

1. **Owner**, Full control. The person who created the room.
2. **Admin**, Almost full control. Can manage channels, members, and settings.
3. **Moderator**, Can moderate members and content.
4. **Member**, The default role. Can read and send messages, join voice channels.

Rooms can customize these roles and add additional ones.

<Info>
  For a full deep-dive on roles and permissions, see [Roles & Permissions](/rooms-and-communities/roles-and-permissions).
</Info>

---

## Public vs. Private Rooms

**Public rooms** are discoverable, they can appear in Tavrn's room discovery and be joined by anyone with a link.

**Private rooms** only accept members via invite link. They don't show up in search or discovery.

Most friend-group rooms are private. Large communities are usually public.

---

## How Big Can a Room Get?

There's no published hard cap on room size. Tavrn is built to scale, and the underlying infrastructure uses proper real-time technology to handle large communities.

---

## Difference Between a Room and a DM

| Room | Direct Message (DM) |
|---|---|
| Community space for many people | Private conversation between 2 people |
| Has multiple channels | Single conversation thread |
| Has roles and permissions | No roles, just two people |
| Can be public or private | Always private |
| Persistent and ongoing | Follows your friendship |
