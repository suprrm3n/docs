# Searching Messages

Your chat history on Tavrn is fully searchable. Whether you're trying to find that link someone sent you last month or tracking down a specific conversation, here's how to use it.

---

## Message Search Basics

Tavrn has a built-in search that lets you search through messages in:
- **DM conversations**, your private chats
- **Room channels**, public messages in channels you have access to

To search:
1. Open the chat or channel you want to search
2. Click the **search icon** (🔍) in the top-right corner of the chat
3. Type your search query
4. Browse the results

<Note>
  Search results are scoped to the conversation or channel you have open. You can't do a platform-wide search across all your DMs and rooms at once (yet).
</Note>

---

## What You Can Search For

- **Keywords**, Any word or phrase that appeared in a message
- **Links**, Paste a URL fragment to find messages containing it
- **Filenames**, Part of a filename to find file attachments
- **Usernames**, Find messages from a specific person (search their username)

---

## Tips for Better Search Results

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Use specific phrases" icon="quote">
    The more specific your search, the better the results. Search for `"meeting at 3pm"` rather than just `meeting`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Try partial words" icon="text">
    If you can't remember the exact word, try the first few letters. Search is flexible.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Search for the file type" icon="file">
    Looking for an audio file someone shared? Try searching for `.mp3` or `.wav`.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Use the media gallery" icon="grid">
    For finding images and media specifically, the **Media Gallery** is often faster than text search.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Searching in the Media Gallery

The Media Gallery (accessible from the top of any chat window) has its own search functionality specifically for files and media. You can filter by:

- **File type** (images, videos, audio)
- **Date range**
- **Filename**

This is particularly useful if you need to find something visual rather than something someone *said*.

<Info>
  Learn more about the Media Gallery in [Sharing Files & Media](/messaging/sharing-files-and-media).
</Info>

---

## Pinned Messages, The Manual "Search"

For frequently referenced content, **pinning** a message is more efficient than searching for it every time.

Pinned messages live in a dedicated panel accessible from the channel header. To pin a message:
1. Hover over the message
2. Click the **⋯ more options** menu
3. Select **Pin Message**

<Note>
  Only room admins and moderators can pin messages in room channels. In DMs, either person can pin messages.
</Note>

---

## Message History & How Far Back You Can Search

All your message history is stored and searchable. There's no cutoff date for standard users.

<Tip>
  **Cocoa members** benefit from extended, unlimited message history, this mainly matters for very active rooms or long-running conversations where history might otherwise be trimmed.
</Tip>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I search across all my DMs at once?">
    Currently, search is scoped to a single conversation or channel. To search across everything, you'd need to open each conversation individually. A global search feature is something the Tavrn team has on the roadmap.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Search isn't returning results I know exist">
    Try different keywords or more general terms. Sometimes the exact phrasing matters. If you're sure a message exists and can't find it, try scrolling the conversation manually, very old messages may take a moment to load.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I search by date?">
    Not directly through the message search at the moment, but you can scroll your message history to a specific time period. The Media Gallery does support date filtering for media files.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Are deleted messages searchable?">
    No. Once a message is deleted, it's gone, it won't appear in search results.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
