# Reactions & Emoji

Tavrn takes emoji seriously. Whether you're reacting to a message or dropping a well-timed 💀 in the chat, here's how it all works.

---

## Reacting to Messages

Reactions let you respond to a message without sending a whole reply. They're great for quick acknowledgment, agreement, or just vibe-confirming that you're reading.

**To add a reaction:**

1. Hover over any message (on desktop) or long-press it (on mobile)
2. Click the 😊 emoji icon that appears
3. Pick your emoji from the picker
4. Your reaction appears below the message

**Reactions are stackable**, if five people all react with 🔥, the message shows `🔥 5`. Multiple emoji can all exist on the same message at once.

---

## Reacting with a Recent Emoji

The reaction picker shows your most recently used emoji at the top for quick access. If you're a creature of habit (👍 on everything, no judgment), this makes repeat-reacting very fast.

---

## Removing a Reaction

Changed your mind? Click your existing reaction again to remove it. It's a toggle.

---

## Viewing Who Reacted

Hover over a reaction count to see a list of who added that reaction. Useful for when you need to know exactly which four people think something is 💀.

---

## The Emoji Picker

The full emoji picker is available everywhere you can type, DMs, room channels, the reaction picker. Click the 😊 button in the message toolbar to open it.

**Inside the picker you'll find:**

- **All standard emoji** organized by category (people, nature, food, travel, objects, symbols, flags...)
- **Recently used**, quick access to your go-tos
- **Search**, type any keyword to find exactly what you're looking for
- **Skin tone selector**, set a default skin tone for applicable emoji

<Tip>
  You can also type emoji codes directly in your message using `:emoji_name:` syntax. For example, `:wave:` → 👋. Start typing `:` followed by a keyword and an autocomplete menu will appear.
</Tip>

---

## Emoji Rendering on Tavrn

Tavrn renders emoji using **Twemoji**, Twitter's open-source emoji set. This means emoji look consistent across all devices, regardless of what your operating system normally renders.

This is actually a big deal! Emoji can look radically different (and sometimes confusing) between iOS, Android, and Windows. Twemoji keeps everyone on the same page.

## GIFs vs. Emoji

Both GIFs and emoji are ways to express yourself, but they serve different purposes:

| | Emoji | GIF |
|---|---|---|
| **Size** | Inline with text | Larger, standalone |
| **Movement** | Static (or animated in some cases) | Animated |
| **Source** | Built-in to Tavrn | NovaGPI search |
| **Use case** | Quick reactions, inline expression | Bigger emotional moments |

<Info>
  Learn more about the GIF picker in [Using GIFs](/messaging/using-gifs).
</Info>

## Tips for Reaction Culture

A few informal community norms around reactions that tend to make conversations feel good:

- **React to things you enjoy**, it costs nothing and makes the sender feel heard
- **Don't over-react**, plastering 50 reactions on every message gets visually noisy
- **Use reactions instead of "haha" or "ok"**, keeps the chat cleaner and quieter
- **Reactions aren't endorsements**, a 😂 reaction just means something made you laugh, not that you agree with everything said

<Note>
  Reactions are visible to everyone in the conversation or channel. In DMs, only you and the other person can see them.
</Note>

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I react with a custom emoji?">
    Currently, Tavrn uses the standard Twemoji set. Custom room emoji aren't yet a feature, but it's something people have asked for!
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I see all reactions on a message?">
    Yes, hover over any reaction to see who added it. You can also scroll through all reactions if there are many on a popular message.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a limit to how many reactions can be on a message?">
    No hard limit. In theory, every emoji in existence could be on a single message. In practice, that would be a lot.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I turn off emoji reactions?">
    You can't disable reactions on messages sent to you (it's part of the core experience), but you can choose not to react to others' messages yourself.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
