# What is Cocoa? ☕

Cocoa is Tavrn's optional premium membership. It's the thing that keeps Tavrn's lights on while keeping the core product free for everyone.

Here's the important part first: **Cocoa never locks away features you need to use Tavrn normally.** Everything that makes Tavrn Tavrn, messaging, rooms, voice calls, friends, all of it, is free forever.

Cocoa adds a layer of extras on top: quality-of-life improvements, cosmetic upgrades, and a few things that just make the experience more fun.

---

## The Name

"Cocoa" fits the Tavrn vibe perfectly, warm, comforting, a little treat. It's a nod to the platform's philosophy: Tavrn should feel like a cozy place, not a cold corporation squeezing you for money.

The brand color is a rich, warm brown (`#c47c3a`), distinct from Tavrn's main orange, giving Cocoa its own identity.

## Why Cocoa Exists

Tavrn is built to be a real alternative to platforms that monetize your attention. No ads, no algorithmic manipulation, no selling your data.

That means Tavrn needs a different revenue model. Cocoa is it, an honest trade: if Tavrn adds value to your life, you can support it and get some nice things in return.

If you can't afford Cocoa or just don't need the extras, that's completely valid. The platform is still for you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens if my Cocoa expires?">
    Your perks deactivate and you return to the standard free experience. Your profile goes back to static avatar, standard status, etc. Nothing is deleted, your data stays put.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I pause my Cocoa subscription?">
    Cocoa supports cancellation at period end, you keep your perks until the billing period expires, then it stops renewing. A full "pause" feature isn't currently available.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is there a Cocoa trial?">
    There may be trial periods offered occasionally. Check the Cocoa page for current promotions.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Does Cocoa support Tavrn's development?">
    Yes. Cocoa revenue goes directly toward keeping the platform running and funding new features. Subscribing is a direct way to support Tavrn.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
